Dear Jalaina

If there’s one thing I’ve learned about “ugly boys,” it’s that they’re rarely told they’re handsome by someone who isn’t a blood relative. Before, I used to think no one could ever love me.

Ever since middle school, I’ve been insecure about my looks and about how people perceived me. Recently, though, I had an important enlightenment: we are often our own worst enemies. The harshest judgments don’t come from others, they come from within ourselves.

So many people believe they’re uglier than they truly are, when in reality they’re not ugly at all. It’s the actions people do that determine true ugliness.

I remember throughout most of middle school, even in 80 degree weather I would wear hoodies just to hide my body. I thought that maybe I could cover it up and the pain it brought would just disappear. Looking back on this period of my life, I cringe so hard.

Meeting you has begun to fill my pessimistic half empty cup to full. I began to have a more optimistic outlook on myself because of you. I'm still learning to truly love myself, so I hope you can forgive me and understand as I am likely to be reluctant to take photos. But I will make an effort, for you and for myself.

So thank you, Jalaina, for putting me on the path toward self love. One day I hope to love myself as much as I love you.
-Jr
